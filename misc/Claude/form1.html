Citi Sanctions & Trade Restrictions Questionnaire1

Citigroup Inc., its subsidiaries, and affiliates, including Citibank, N.A., (collectively, “Citi”) are required to comply with applicable sanctions laws and regulations

(“Sanctions”) imposed and administered by the United States, the United Kingdom, United Nations, and the European Union as well as by other sanctions

authorities of each jurisdiction where Citi operates.

As a Citi client, you and your branches/offices and subsidiaries should refrain from directing transactions to or through Citi and/or otherwise utilizing Citi’s

products, funds, accounts, or services in connection with any transaction or activity, directly or indirectly involving “Sanctioned Parties” which for the purposes

of this document refers to any of the following if they are subject to sanctions: individuals, entities, sanctioned governments, restricted goods and software,

professional services, aircraft, or vessels as well as “Sanctioned Jurisdictions” (Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Syria, Belarus, Russia, Venezuela, and the occupied

territories/non-government-controlled territories of Ukraine (Crimea, Donetsk, Kherson, Luhansk, and Zaporizhzhia), or where such transaction or activity

would be prohibited for Citi, and/or would result in a violation of Sanctions by Citi.

Through this questionnaire, Citi seeks to understand the extent of your activities with Sanctioned Parties, Sanctioned Jurisdictions, or other potentially prohibited

activity, including those activities that do not use Citi products and services. Please provide information on your activities in or with “Sanctioned Jurisdictions”

and/or “Sanctioned Parties.”

accurate responses and ensure it relates to current or contemplated activities. If any information is unclear or incomplete, we may need to reach out for

Glossary of Terms

Beneficial Owner

A beneficial owner is any natural person or entity that can exercise control, directly or indirectly, over the account holder including through control over the

accountholder’s account(s) or non-account products or services.

Direct Exposure

Engaging in prohibited transactions or dealings directly with individuals, entities, or countries subject to sanctions. For example, physical assets, employees,

customers, suppliers, active contracts, and/or transactions to or from a sanctioned jurisdiction.

EU/UK Specified Regions (EUSR)

For the purposes of this questionnaire, EUSR are EU/UK Specified Regions of Crimea, Donetsk, Kherson, Luhansk, and Zaporizhzhia.

Indirect Exposure

Engaging in prohibited transactions or dealings with individuals, entities, or countries subject to sanctions through a third party or intermediary. For example, a

customer’s customer, any party within the supply chain, a company acting on behalf of another entity (such as a shell company), or transactions through a third party

such as another bank / correspondent bank. Indirect sanctions exposure can also occur through limited ownership of a sanctioned entity (for example, an entity non-

controlled and 5% owned by a sanctioned party may be legally permissible, but there sanctions exposure exists.

New Investments

Examples of new investment include but may not be limited to: (1) entry into an agreement requiring the commitment of capital or other assets for the establishment

or expansion of projects or operations in Russia, including the formation of joint ventures or other corporate entities in Russia; (2) entry into an agreement providing

for the participation in royalties or ongoing profits in Russia; (3) the lending of funds to persons located in Russia for commercial purposes, including when such funds

are intended to be used to fund a new or expanded project or operation in Russia; (4) the purchase of real estate, rights to natural resources or exploitation thereof,

or an equity interest in an entity, located in the Russia.

Sanctioned Jurisdictions

For the purposes of this questionnaire, the sanctioned jurisdictions are Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Syria, Belarus, Russia, Venezuela, and the occupied territories/non-

government-controlled territories of Ukraine (Crimea, Donetsk, Kherson, Luhansk, and Zaporizhzhia).

Sanctioned Parties

For the purposes of this questionnaire, sanctioned parties are referred to as: individuals, entities, sanctioned governments, restricted goods and software,

professional services, aircraft, or vessels that are the subject of sanctions designations, including the OFAC, EU, UK or UN lists, or lists relevant to the local

jurisdiction.
